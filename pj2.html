TritonEd Website

TritonEd Website redesign is a two month project in which I, in conjunction with three other people, find problems with the current unfriendly website(shown below), conduct research to understand what users want and implement those changes.

I conducted many interviews and watched my subjects perform given tasks such as check assignment or check due date. We used Google Form to collect what was not so efficient in current website and I helped to write some of the questions to contextualize problems users had. This process proved to be very crucial as data we collected served to guide us in the design stage.

The final version is the result of relocation of features that emerged useful during interviews, integration of suggestions mentioned in the Google Form and beautification of interfaces originally on the website. I suggested that we should incorprate some mobile design scheme into our website design because users are becoming incresingly fammiliar with the mobile design language due to the excessive amount of exposure with mobile applications.

This idea was carried out with a three dash hidden menu at the top left corner. The menu contains some of the secondary functions which will get used but not so often. Also at the bottom right calender function, a pop-up displaying exams, quizes and due dates window will show up whenever hovering on a certain date. The main page is given full consideration and optimization for student usage and if adopted, will no doubt increase the efficiency on the page and reduce frustration.
