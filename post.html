Triton Park

Triton Park is a two month project that features UCSD on campus parking lot finding and parking data viewing. It is a start from sratch project and below are our storyboards, paper prototype and low fidelity digital prototype:

From there, we went on to the real website. I was involved with the entire design decision alongwith three other people. The app begins directly with home screen but there is a tutorial by clicking "How do I use Triton Park?" button at the bottom of home page. See pictures below:

In main page, user can enter where they are going their parking permit type and app will display suggestions and their availability status. I proposed the idea of classifying different states of parking lot because I think classification will create a better sense of which parking lot to go since data visualization is better. In light of that proposition: blue means great; yellow means average and red means stay away from that parking lotï¼š

After selecting a parking lot from the screen, user can see the availability from a graph and could further go into an interactive plot of remaining parking spot. I decided to incorprate an interactive plot to add a layer of visualization of the app.
